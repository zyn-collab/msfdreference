## Internal Inconsistencies

| Item | Inconsistency | Details |
|------|--------------|---------|
| A | **Life expectancy figures** | [^1]/[^5] cite 81.1 years (UN DESA WPP 2022); [^116]/[^234] cite 79.6 years (2019, National Ageing Policy 2024); [^319] "Ageing Ungracefully" cites 87 years (apparent error). The 81.1 and 79.6 figures are reconcilable (79.6 in 2019 rising to 81.1 by 2023), but the 87-year figure appears erroneous. |
| B | **Home Visit Programme patient count** | [^216] (MoGFSS 2018 report): 93 registered patients. [^41] (Taj and Suzana 2023, data from 2022): 67 patients (37 Dhamanaveshi + 30 Hulhumale). [^360] (MIPAA Survey 2021): 77 patients. Different reporting periods and possible attrition explain the variation. |
| C | **Elderly population thresholds** | Multiple sources use different age thresholds interchangeably: 60+ (~43,000 / 8.3%) and 65+ (~20,553 / 5%). The MIPAA Statement projects 12.7% aged 65+ by 2045; MoSFD projects 34.1% aged 60+ by 2054. The different thresholds (60+ vs 65+) must be distinguished when comparing projections. |
| D | **Ageing society threshold year** | [^198]/[^129]: "ageing society" by 2031. Some sources use 7% aged 65+ as the threshold; others report the Maldives already at 8.3% aged 60+. The threshold definition (which age group) determines the timeline. |
| E | **TFR starting point** | [^1]/[^4]: TFR 6.4 in 1990. [^334]: TFR "from 6" in 1995. The 6.4 figure is from Census 1990; the "6" figure from Corporate Maldives (2025) appears to be a rounded approximation using 1995 as baseline rather than 1990. |
