# PART VIII: SUBSTANCE ABUSE (08_Substance_Abuse.md)

1. "Drug abuse was not a recognised phenomenon in Maldivian society before the mid-1970s. Although anecdotal accounts reference limited opium use in the early twentieth century, these cases were confined to small numbers of individuals and did not constitute a public health concern."
   - **Source**: Shazly, M. & Zilaal, H., "Drug Offender Treatment in the Maldives," *Resource Material Series No. 85*, UNAFEI 148th International Training Course, p. 27.
   - **Link**: None

2. "The appearance of drug abuse in its modern form coincided with the introduction of tourism in the early 1970s. Hundreds of tourists began arriving daily from Europe, Southeast Asia, and South Asia."
   - **Source**: Shazly, M. & Zilaal, H., "Drug Offender Treatment in the Maldives," *Resource Material Series No. 85*, UNAFEI 148th International Training Course, p. 27.
   - **Link**: None

3. "Official recognition of the drug problem came in 1977 when a person was arrested with 350 grams of hashish. This event triggered the passage of the first principal legislative act dealing with narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances: Law No. 17/77 -- The Law on Drugs."
   - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 27; National Drug Agency & WHO, *Situational Analysis of Drugs in the Maldives 2021*, p. 16.
   - **Link**: None

4. "The situation escalated in 1993 with the detection of the first case of heroin abuse."
   - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 28.
   - **Link**: None

5. "By 1996, a total of 241 cases of substance abuse were reported to the police. In 1998, over 450 arrests were made for drug abuse and related offences."
   - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 28.
   - **Link**: None

6. "The first major seizure of cocaine occurred in September 1993 at Male International Airport, when eight kilograms of cocaine were found concealed in the false bottom of a suitcase carried by a foreign national."
   - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 28.
   - **Link**: None

7. "In 1997, three Maldivians were discovered attempting to smuggle 1,372 grams of hashish oil in seven professionally packed cans of corned beef while boarding a flight from Trivandrum Airport to Male."
   - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 28.
   - **Link**: None

8. "The government responded to the escalating crisis by establishing the Narcotics Control Board (NCB) in 1997, alongside the Drug Rehabilitation Centre (DRC) on the island of Himafushi."
   - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 29; Mortimore, G. & Stimson, G., *A Process and Systems Evaluation of the Drug Treatment and Rehabilitation Services in the Maldives*, World Bank, November 2010, p. 20.
   - **Link**: None

9. "By the early 2000s, the Maldivian authorities reported that 'the problem of drug-related offences [had] become the most frequent one faced by the Maldivian criminal courts, showing a 200% increase in recent years.'"

   - **Link**: None

10. "The majority of drug abusers were young people aged 16 to 30. For a country where more than 50% of the population was below 16 years of age, this represented an alarming demographic trajectory."
    - **Source**: Shazly & Zilaal, op. cit., p. 27, quoting MDV, 2001.
    - **Link**: None
