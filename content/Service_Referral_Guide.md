# Service Referral Guide

---

## Section 1: How to Use This Directory

### Purpose

This directory is designed as a practical reference tool for case management teams, social workers, island council staff, and community social groups operating across the Maldives. Its primary function is to answer a single operational question: **"Someone on Island X needs Service Y – what are their options, both locally and at the nearest hub?"**

The Maldives' extreme geographic dispersal – 187 inhabited islands spread across 26 natural atolls, covering approximately 90,000 square kilometres of ocean – means that no single service model can serve every location equally. A person in Male' has access to the full spectrum of social, health, legal, and protection services within walking distance or a short ferry ride. A person on a remote island in Haa Alifu Atoll may need to travel for hours by speedboat, or days by scheduled ferry, to reach equivalent services. This directory maps that reality so that frontline workers can provide realistic referral guidance.

### How to Navigate

1. **Start with the presenting need.** Identify what category of service the person requires: child protection, domestic violence response, health care, mental health, drug rehabilitation, disability services, elderly care, legal assistance, or social protection benefits.

2. **Identify the person's location.** Determine their island and administrative atoll.

3. **Check the atoll-level entry** in Section 5 for locally available services – the FCSC, health facility, police station, magistrate court, and any CSOs operating in the area.

4. **If the needed service is not available locally**, check the nearest **regional service hub** in Section 4, which lists the five regional hospital areas and their full service complement.

5. **For services available only in Male'**, refer to Section 3 for the Greater Male' Area directory.

6. **For services accessible from anywhere** (helplines, online portals, national agencies), refer to Section 2.

7. **For referral pathways** for common scenarios (child abuse, DV, disability assessment, drug treatment, mental health crisis, elderly care), refer to Section 7.

### Understanding Service Tiers

The Maldivian service delivery system operates on a tiered model across all sectors:

| Tier | Health | Social Services | Justice | Education |
|------|--------|----------------|---------|-----------|
| **Tier 4: National/Central** | IGMH, ADK, Tree Top, Hulhumale' Hospital | MoSFD, CFPS, FPA, NSPA (all in Male') | Supreme Court, High Court, Family Court, Drug Court, Juvenile Court | MNU, IUM, Maldives Polytechnic |
| **Tier 3: Regional** | 5 regional hospitals | FCSC in atoll capital + regional coordination | High Court branches (Male', Addu, Kulhudhuffushi) | Higher secondary schools, satellite campuses |
| **Tier 2: Atoll** | 14 atoll hospitals | FCSC (1 per atoll) | Magistrate courts on inhabited islands | Government schools (219 nationally) |
| **Tier 1: Island** | Health centres (169+) on inhabited islands | Island council, WDC, community social groups | Magistrate court (if island population 500+) | Primary/secondary school |

### Key Principle for Referrals

**Always exhaust local options first.** Travel between islands is expensive, time-consuming, and weather-dependent. For a vulnerable person – a DV survivor with children, an elderly person with mobility limitations, a person with a disability – the logistical burden of inter-island travel can be prohibitive. Effective case management requires knowing what can be accessed locally and what genuinely requires referral to a higher tier.

### 2.3 National Agencies

These agencies are headquartered in Male' but administer programmes and exercise authority nationwide.

#### Ministry of Social and Family Development (MoSFD)
- **Mandate:** Policy formulation and service delivery for child protection, gender equality and GBV, social welfare for elderly and persons with disabilities, and community-level social service coordination through FCSCs.[^4]
- **Key units:** Child and Family Protection Services (CFPS), Policy Department, Social Protection and Services Division, Gender Equality Division.
- **Previous names:** Ministry of Gender, Family and Social Services (MoGFSS, 2018-2023); Ministry of Gender and Family (2013-2018).

#### National Social Protection Agency (NSPA)
- **Established:** 2008 (initially as department; later upgraded)
- **Programmes administered:** Aasandha universal health insurance (via Aasandha Company Ltd); disability allowance (MVR 2,000-7,000/month); single parent allowance (MVR 1,000/month per child, max MVR 3,000); foster parent allowance; food assistance; Disability Therapy Assistance Programme.[^5]
- **Key system:** Social Protection Information System (SPIS); National Disability Registry.
- **Website:** nspa.gov.mv

#### Child and Family Protection Services (CFPS)
- **Parent body:** MoSFD
- **Mandate:** Operational child protection casework at national level. Manages all 20 atoll-level FCSCs. Specialised teams for assessment, investigation, legal liaison, and case coordination.[^6]
- **Data system:** Maldives Child Protection Database (MCPD, launched 2016)
- **Helpline:** 1412 (toll-free, 24-hour)

#### Family Protection Authority (FPA)
- **Established:** Under DVPA 2012
- **Mandate:** Central coordinating body for domestic violence response: receives and responds to DV reports, coordinates multi-agency response, maintains DV database, conducts public awareness, monitors compliance, advises government.[^7]

#### National Drug Agency (NDA)
- **Established:** Under Drugs Act (Act 17/2011), replacing Narcotics Control Board
- **Mandate:** Drug prevention, treatment, rehabilitation, data collection, treatment standards, laboratory services.
- **Facilities:** 10 treatment facilities established (7 operational as of 2021).[^8]

#### Human Rights Commission of the Maldives (HRCM)
- **Mandate:** Independent statutory body promoting and protecting human rights. Investigates complaints, inspects detention and care facilities, submits shadow reports to UN treaty bodies.[^9]
- **Website:** hrcm.org.mv

#### Children's Ombudsperson
- **Established:** 2020 (under CRPA 2019)
- **Mandate:** Independent office investigating complaints related to children's rights violations, monitoring CRPA compliance, making recommendations to government and Majlis, conducting child rights audits across atolls.[^10]

#### Maldives Pension Administration Office (MPAO)
- **Mandate:** Administers Old-Age Basic Pension (MVR 5,000/month for citizens 65+) and the Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme (MRPS – defined contribution, mandatory for all formal sector employees).
- **Assets under management:** Over MVR 15 billion (2023).[^11]

## Section 3: Male' / Greater Male' Area Services

The Greater Male' Area – comprising Male' City, Hulhumale', and Villimale' – contains approximately 47% of the national population and the overwhelming majority of specialist services. For many social service needs, referral to Male' remains the only option for residents of outer atolls.

**Administrative Unit:** Male' City, Hulhumale', Villimale'
**Population:** Approximately 252,000 (47% of national population)
**Islands:** Male' City, Hulhumale' (Phase 1 and 2), Villimale'

### 3.1 Government Social Services

- **CFPS (Central Office)** – Child and Family Protection Services headquarters, within MoSFD, Male' City. National-level case management, assessment, investigation, legal liaison. Manages all 20 atoll-level FCSCs. Helpline: **1412** (24/7, toll-free).
- **Family Protection Authority (FPA)** – Central coordinating body for all domestic violence response nationwide. Receives DV reports, coordinates multi-agency response, maintains DV database. Male' City.[^7]
- **Amaan Hiya (Safe House)** – Villimale'. Emergency shelter for DV victims and their children, operated by MoSFD.[^18]
- **Fiyavathi (Children's Home)** – Hulhumale'. Established 2016, funded by Government of India (MVR 1.9 million renovation under Indian grant). Capacity approximately 130 children. Serves children below 13 years who have been abandoned, neglected, deprived, or abused and placed in state care.[^16]
- **Kudakudhinge Hiya (Children's Home)** – Villimale'. Approximately 50 children. Serves children aged 13-18 years in state care.[^17]
- **MPS Family and Child Protection Department** – Male' headquarters. Phone: 979 0163. Investigation of DV, child abuse, sexual offences against children.[^20]
- **Children's Ombudsperson** – Independent office established 2020 under CRPA 2019. Investigates children's rights violations, conducts child rights audits across atolls.[^10]

### 3.2 Health Facilities

#### Tertiary Hospitals

**Indira Gandhi Memorial Hospital (IGMH)**
- Male' City. Government tertiary referral hospital, largest in the Maldives.
- Specialties: Cardiology, neurology, nephrology, oncology, orthopaedics, paediatrics, obstetrics/gynaecology, emergency/trauma, psychiatry.
- **National Centre for Mental Health (NCMH):** Inaugurated 2019. Outpatient psychiatric consultations, inpatient psychiatric care (padded observation rooms opened 2022). Psychiatry waitlists reduced from 3,300+ to approximately 330 (90% reduction) by 2023.[^12]
- **Family Protection Unit:** Receives DV and sexual violence cases presenting for medical care.
- Ambulance: **102**

**ADK Hospital**
- Male' City. Private tertiary hospital, 50 beds.
- Specialties: Orthopaedics, gastroenterology, minimally invasive surgery, ophthalmology, cardiology, diagnostic imaging. Department of Psychiatry providing treatment and prevention of mental health disorders.[^13]
- First in Maldives to offer IVF services (2016).
- Ambulance: 3313553
- **ADK@Home:** Home healthcare service for elderly and post-operative patients.

**Tree Top Hospital**
- Hulhumale'. Private tertiary hospital, 210 beds.
- 35+ specialised departments including cardiology, gastroenterology, neurology, oncology, paediatrics. First in Maldives for open-heart surgery. Doctors from 35+ countries.[^14]
- **Fertility Centre:** Nation's first dedicated fertility centre, opened 2023.
- Contact: 3035186, executiveoffice@treetophospital.com

**Hulhumale' Hospital**
- Government hospital, tertiary-level services.
- **Centre for Mental Health (CMH):** Established October 2022 in collaboration with Cadabams Group (India). Comprehensive diagnosis, treatment, specialised care, therapy sessions.[^15]
- Dialysis services available.

### 3.14 Referral Notes

Male' is the national referral destination for all specialist services. The Greater Male' Area has no service gaps – all tiers of social, health, legal, and protection services are accessible. The challenge in Male' is capacity (FCSC caseloads averaging 160+ per worker), not availability.[^72]

**Specific capacity constraints in Male':**
- IGMH NCMH: While waitlists reduced from 3,300+ to approximately 330 (a 90% reduction by 2023), demand continues to grow. Only centre with inpatient psychiatric beds.
- Fiyavathi (Hulhumale'): Operating over capacity at approximately 130 children against designed capacity of approximately 80.
- DRC Himmafushi: Optimum 150, maximum 250 – regularly stretched beyond capacity. Women's dormitory limited to 20 beds.
- Prosecutor General's Office: Only 3% of DV/GBV cases submitted for prosecution; 16.7% conviction rate. Many survivors disengage from legal processes.
- Foster care: Only 18 registered foster carers nationally (as of 2023).[^71]

## Section 4: Regional Service Hubs

The five regional hospitals serve as anchor institutions for social service delivery outside Male'. Each regional hospital serves a cluster of 2-4 administrative atolls, providing the most advanced health care available outside the capital. Since 2019, all five have been elevated to tertiary status with specialist departments, and all five have been staffed with psychiatrists – a transformative development for mental health access.[^36]

### 4.1 Northern Region – Kulhudhuffushi Regional Hospital (Haa Dhaalu Atoll)

**Location:** Kulhudhuffushi, Haa Dhaalu Atoll (Kulhudhuffushi City)

**Atolls served:** Haa Alifu (HA), Haa Dhaalu (HDh), Shaviyani (Sh)

**Health services:**
- Tertiary-level care with specialist departments
- Chemotherapy services (oncology-trained nurses and experienced doctors)
- Dialysis services for kidney conditions
- Intensive Critical Care Unit (ICCU)
- Neonatal Intensive Care Unit (NICU)
- Modern operation theatres (elective and emergency)
- Labour and delivery rooms
- Methadone rehabilitation services for opioid dependence[^37]
- Mental health services: psychiatrist on staff; telepsychiatry video consultations available for islands in HDh Atoll

**Social services available in Kulhudhuffushi:**
- FCSC – Haa Dhaalu Atoll Family and Children Service Centre
- Amaan Veshi safe house (planned/established for DV survivors)
- Police station (Maldives Police Service)
- High Court branch (one of three in the country)
- Magistrate court
- Government schools
- WDC
- Island/city council

## Section 7: Referral Pathways

This section provides quick-reference guidance for the most common referral scenarios that case management teams encounter. Each pathway describes the sequence of actions from initial identification through to service access, identifying the responsible agencies and contact points at each stage.

### 7.1 Child Abuse Reported on an Outer Island

**Scenario:** A teacher, health worker, or community member on an outer island identifies signs of child abuse (physical, sexual, emotional, or neglect).

| Step | Action | Responsible Agency | Contact |
|------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Report immediately** | Reporter (mandatory for all persons under CRPA 2019) | Call **1412** (child protection helpline) AND/OR report to local FCSC social worker |
| 2 | **If child is in immediate danger**, contact police | Maldives Police Service | Call **119** or local police station |
| 3 | **FCSC conducts initial assessment** | FCSC social worker on atoll capital | Travels to island if not resident; may take hours/days depending on weather |
| 4 | **If emergency removal is necessary**, FCSC takes child into state care | FCSC / CFPS (Male') | Application for Interim Assessment Order (Article 80 CRPA) must be filed within 5 days |
| 5 | **CFPS in Male' provides case coordination** | CFPS centralised team | Updates case in Maldives Child Protection Database (MCPD) |
| 6 | **Medical examination if required** | Atoll hospital or nearest health facility | Refer to regional hospital if specialist examination needed |
| 7 | **Police investigation** if criminal offence suspected | MPS Family and Child Protection Department | Local police; coordination with central FCPW in Male' |
| 8 | **Alternative care placement** if child cannot remain with family | CFPS | Options: kinship care, foster care (only 18 registered foster carers nationally as of 2023), or residential care (Fiyavathi for under-13s, Kudakudhinge Hiya for 13-18, Amaan Veshi facilities)[^71] |
| 9 | **Court proceedings** | Family Court (Male') / Magistrate Court | Protection Order or Supervision Order as appropriate |

**Critical gap to note:** FCSC workers carry average caseloads of 160+ cases per worker.[^72] Response times to outer islands depend on boat availability and weather conditions. During rough weather, response may be delayed for days.

**Scenario:** A woman experiencing domestic violence on any island needs immediate safe shelter.

| Step | Action | Responsible Agency | Contact |
|------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Report to police** (mandatory for all persons under DVPA 2012) | Maldives Police Service | Call **119** or local police station |
| 2 | **Police respond** – may enter residence without warrant to ensure safety | MPS | Police notify FPA of every DV complaint |
| 3 | **Request FCSC social worker presence** | FCSC / FPA | Police request social worker at scene |
| 4 | **Emergency Protection Order** – can be issued ex parte | Courts | Temporary Protection Order prohibiting perpetrator contact, excluding from residence |
| 5 | **Safe housing options:** | | |
| 5a | *If Amaan Veshi available in atoll:* | MoSFD | Amaan Veshi safe houses located in: HA. Dhidhdhoo, Sh. Funadhoo, B. Eydhafushi, Lh. Naifaru, M. Mulah, Dh. Kudahuvadhoo, Th. Veymandoo, GDh. Thinadhoo, Gn. Fuvahmulah, S. Hithadhoo[^73] |
| 5b | *If no Amaan Veshi in atoll:* | FCSC / FPA | Options: relocation to family member's island; transfer to Amaan Hiya (Villimale'); arrangement with island council for temporary housing |
| 6 | **Legal assistance** | Family Legal Clinic | Hotline: 9977771 (working days 9am-5pm). Pro bono legal advice on protection orders, custody, maintenance, divorce |

### 7.3 Person with Disability Needs Assessment and Benefits

**Scenario:** A person with a disability (or their family) on any island needs to obtain disability registration and benefits.

| Step | Action | Responsible Agency | Contact |
|------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Obtain medical documentation** of disability | Nearest health facility (HC, atoll hospital, or regional hospital) | GP certificate may suffice for some conditions; specialist documentation may be demanded for others |
| 2 | **If specialist assessment required:** | Regional hospital or IGMH | Travel to Male' or regional hospital; psychiatrist assessment for psychosocial disabilities particularly difficult to obtain in atolls[^75] |
| 3 | **Submit application** via NDR Portal | NSPA | ndr.nspa.gov.mv – portal is not in accessible format; family assistance usually required. Some island councils assist. |
| 4 | **Application reviewed** by NSPA committee | NSPA / MoSFD | Processing delays of up to 12 months reported.[^76] Status communicated via text message (not accessible for deaf/visually impaired). |
| 5 | **If approved:** Receive disability allowance | NSPA | Tiered: basic MVR 2,000 + general additional MVR 1,000 = MVR 3,000 minimum; up to MVR 7,000/month for those requiring full carer support |
| 6 | **Disability identification card** | NSPA | Provides priority healthcare (inconsistent), free public transport (buses and RTL ferries) |
| 7 | **Therapy assistance** | NSPA Disability Therapy Assistance Programme | Financial support for physiotherapy, speech therapy, occupational therapy, behavioural therapy. Application required; partial cost coverage. |
| 8 | **Education support** for children | Ministry of Education | SEN units in 87% of government schools; however, reasonable accommodations are often absent[^77] |

**Critical gap to note:** Only 25.6% of persons with disabilities receive the disability allowance. 44% of Census-identified PWDs (10,745 persons) are not registered in the NDR.[^78]

### 7.4 Elderly Person Needs Home Care or Residential Support

**Scenario:** An elderly person on any island needs assistance with daily living, health care, or residential placement.

| Step | Action | Responsible Agency | Contact |
|------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Confirm pension eligibility** | MPAO | All citizens aged 65+ eligible for Old-Age Basic Pension (MVR 5,000/month). Non-means-tested, universal. |
| 2 | **Health care access** | Nearest health facility | All citizens covered by Aasandha universal health insurance. No premiums, co-payments, or deductibles. Free referral to nearest hospital including emergency sea transport. |
| 3 | **Home-based care** | Family / Island council / FCSC | **No formal home care programme exists nationally.** Care depends on family members. Island councils may provide informal support. |
| 4 | **If residential care required** | MoSFD | The only facility housing elderly: HPSN Guraidhoo (K. Guraidhoo). Not a dedicated elderly facility; 219 residents including psychiatric patients.[^79] |
| 5 | **Community support** | WDC / Island council | AgedCare Maldives (NGO) conducting activities with MoSFD MoU |
| 6 | **If elder abuse suspected** | FCSC / Police | Report under DVPA 2012 (domestic violence provisions). No specific elder abuse reporting framework exists.[^80] |

**Critical gap to note:** No dedicated elderly care residential facilities exist in the Maldives. No dedicated elderly law has been enacted. No formal home care programme exists. The National Plan of Action on Older Persons 2019-2023 was severely disrupted by COVID-19, and "no monitoring has been done since the pandemic situation."[^81]

### 7.6 Mental Health Crisis

**Scenario:** A person on any island is experiencing a mental health crisis (severe psychiatric symptoms, suicidal ideation, psychotic episode).

| Step | Action | Responsible Agency | Contact |
|------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | **Immediate safety** | Family / Community / Police (if danger to self or others) | Call **119** if immediate risk |
| 2 | **Contact nearest health facility** | Island health centre or atoll hospital | Basic assessment by nurse or GP; mhGAP-trained workers can identify and manage common conditions |
| 3 | **Telepsychiatry consultation** (if available) | Regional hospital psychiatrist via video | Available in L. Atoll, Th. Atoll, HDh. Atoll, R. Atoll, GDh. Atoll – approximately 55,000 people across 56 islands can access within 2 weeks[^85] |
| 4 | **If in-person psychiatric care required:** | Regional hospital or IGMH | Transfer to nearest facility with psychiatrist. All 5 regional hospitals have psychiatrists; collectively 150-200 consultations/week |
| 5 | **If psychiatric inpatient admission required:** | IGMH National Centre for Mental Health | Male' – inpatient wards opened 2022 with padded observation rooms |
| 6 | **If substance use is a factor:** | NDA / Regional hospital | Dual diagnosis assessment; refer to appropriate treatment pathway |
| 7 | **Follow-up care** | Regional hospital outreach / Island health centre | Psychiatrists from regional hospitals conduct outreach visits to smaller islands. Medication management and follow-up. |
| 8 | **School-based** (if young person) | School counsellor / Mental health Early Intervention programme | Programmes operating through Hulhumale' Hospital and Dr Abdul Samad Memorial Hospital (Thinadhoo)[^86] |

**Critical gap to note:** No standalone Mental Health Act exists. No legal framework for involuntary commitment, emergency psychiatric holds, or community treatment orders.[^87] Average waiting time for IGMH psychiatry appointment approximately 2 months (down from 18+ months). Telepsychiatry coverage remains partial – many atolls do not yet have access.

### Critical Findings

1. **FCSCs are universal** – all 20 administrative atolls have one. But staffing remains severely inadequate (160+ caseload per worker).

2. **Safe houses/shelters** exist in 12-14 locations but capacity data is not publicly available for most. Several are listed as "planned/established" with unclear operational status.

3. **Children's residential care** (Fiyavathi) exists in only 6 locations: Hulhumale', HA. Dhidhdhoo, Sh. Funadhoo, N. Manadhoo, B. Eydhafushi, Gn. Fuvahmulah. Children from other atolls requiring state care must be separated from their communities.

4. **Drug treatment** is concentrated: only 6 operational NDA facilities (Himmafushi, Hanimaadhoo, Thinadhoo, Fuvahmulah, Hithadhoo, Villingili) with combined capacity of approximately 570. Three additional facilities are under construction (Velidhoo, Kaashidhoo, Gan). **No women-specific detox outside the 20-bed dormitory at DRC Himmafushi.**

5. **Psychiatric services** are available at only 5 regional hospitals (Kulhudhuffushi, Ungoofaaru, Gan, Thinadhoo, Hithadhoo) plus Male'. Telepsychiatry extends reach to approximately 55,000 people across 56 islands. The Mental Health Strategic Action Plan 2025 (validated December 2024) prioritises integrating mental health into primary healthcare and expanding psychiatrist/psychologist training nationwide.[^118]

6. **Child Development Centres** exist in only 2 locations outside Male': Kulhudhuffushi RH (northern) and SCDC Maradhoo-Feydhoo (southern). Central atolls have no CDC access.

7. **Disability therapy** is almost exclusively Male'-based. Care Society operates CBR in 5 atoll locations (Addu, Thinadhoo, Keyodhoo/Felidhoo, Hanimaadhoo, Maarandhoo). Only 25.6% of PWDs receive the disability allowance; 44% are not registered in NDR.

8. **Elderly care** has the most severe gap: no dedicated residential facility exists anywhere. HPSN Guraidhoo is the default and was not designed for elderly care. No formal home care programme. No dedicated elderly law.

9. **Legal aid** is available only from Family Legal Clinic (Male'-based, extended to Addu). The rest of the country has no pro bono legal service. Magistrate Courts cover 194 locations.

10. **Civil society** is concentrated in Male' (348 social-sector CSOs) and varies dramatically across atolls: Faafu has only 3 social-sector CSOs while Laamu has 40 and Gnaviyani has 21.
