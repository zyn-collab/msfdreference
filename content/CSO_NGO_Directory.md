## 1. National-Level Social Sector CSO Profiles

The following organizations are the most significant CSOs working on social policy issues in the Maldives. These profiles are based on web research, organizational websites, and references in policy documents.

### Hope for Women (HFW)

**Registration:** Registered 2010  
**Focus Area:** Gender-Based Violence, Women's Rights  
**Phone:** 7456570  
**Email:** hope@hopeforwomen.org.mv  
**Website:** hopeforwomen.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Headquartered in Male'; national advocacy reach

Hope for Women is a leading GBV advocacy organization working towards ending all forms of violence against women and domestic violence, while promoting gender equality and women's participation in public life. HFW raises issues of gender injustice and violence against women, working alongside policy makers and stakeholders towards productive changes. Their work addresses the finding that 1 in every 3 Maldivian women between ages 15-49 has experienced physical and/or sexual violence. Activities are mostly project-based with funding from individuals and organizations.

**Key Programs:**
- GBV advocacy and awareness campaigns
- Policy advocacy on domestic violence and gender equality
- Collaboration with Family Legal Clinic on legal aid
- Community outreach on violence against women

### Society for Health Education (SHE)

**Registration:** Founded 1988  
**Focus Area:** Thalassemia, Sexual and Reproductive Health, Counselling  
**Phone:** 3322563  
**Email:** she.maldives@she.org.mv  
**Website:** she.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'; outreach to outer islands

Founded in 1988 by four women, SHE is one of the largest and most vibrant NGOs in the Maldives. It addresses issues concerning Thalassemia, Counselling and Psychosocial Support, Sexual and Reproductive Health (SRH), and Health Education. SHE is a member association of the International Planned Parenthood Federation (IPPF). SHE reaches over 5,000 people yearly.

**Key Programs:**
- Thalassemia Prevention Programme with the only DNA testing facility in the country (est. 2005)
- Counselling and Psychosocial Services: face-to-face counselling, telephone counselling, play therapy for children
- Sexual and Reproductive Health clinic services
- Outreach programmes in outer islands
- Skill development programmes for youth, vulnerable populations, teachers, and parents

### Family Legal Clinic (FLC)

**Registration:** Founded 2014  
**Focus Area:** Legal Aid, Family Law, Domestic Violence  
**Phone:** 7798898 / Hotline: 9977771  
**Website:** familylegalclinic.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'

The first organisation in the Maldives to provide pro bono legal aid. Founded in 2014 by two lawyers and a journalist, in collaboration with Hope for Women. FLC provides free legal counseling on family law, prevention of domestic violence law, and workplace sexual harassment. The team consists of Co-Chairperson, two Legal Officers, and a Programme Officer. Hotline operates 9am-4pm every working day.

**Key Programs:**
- Pro bono legal aid on family law
- Legal support for domestic violence cases
- Workplace sexual harassment legal counselling
- Legal awareness and education

### Moms Aid

**Registration:** Founded 2019
**Focus Area:** Anti-poverty, violence against women, women's empowerment
**Website:** momsaid.com.mv
**Social Media:** Facebook: @MomsAidmv (~37,690 likes); Instagram: @moms__aid; LinkedIn: /company/momsngo
**Geographic Reach:** National (Male'-based operations with island outreach)

**Leadership:** Executive Director: Fathimath Riyasa; Director of Operations: Fathmath Zain Zareer; Director of Administrations: Fathimath Fiureen.

**Vision:** To reach, guide, and motivate women across the Maldives towards a society where domestic abuse will not be tolerated and women can live free from the threat of violence and poverty.

**Mission:** Ending violence and fighting poverty so that all women can create the future they want. Provides immediate and long-term assistance to vulnerable families.

**Key Programmes:**
- **Ramadan Aid** (annual, launched 2020): Distribution of food baskets with essential staples to verified vulnerable families. Reached 178 families through Dusit partnership in 2025; targeting 300 families with EyeCare Maldives in 2026; aiming for 500 families total.
- **Family Sponsorship Program:** Ongoing daily support to verified vulnerable families under Moms Aid care.
- **Back to School:** Essential school supplies for students from financially vulnerable families. BML donated MVR 100,000 in 2025; Dhiraagu partnered for 2025 drive.
- **Domestic violence support:** Temporary shelter for women escaping abuse; referrals to mental health services; skills training for independent livelihoods.
- **Community education:** Awareness campaigns on violence against women, associated risks, and methods to support those in vulnerable situations.

**Corporate partnerships:** Dhiraagu (Ramadan Aid, Back to School), Dusit Thani Maldives, EyeCare Maldives, Maagiri Hotel (80 Iftar packs), Bank of Maldives (MVR 100,000 donation), Villa College (MOU for legal rights awareness, mental health awareness, and need-based skills training for mothers).

**Sources:** momsaid.com.mv; Dhiraagu press releases 2025; Island Chief (islandchief.com); Hotelier Maldives; Villa College MOU announcement.

### Advocating the Rights of Children (ARC)

**Registration:** Established December 2009  
**Focus Area:** Child Protection, Children's Rights  
**Phone:** 7799544  
**Email:** info@arc.org.mv  
**Website:** arc.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'; national programs

ARC works to protect and promote children's rights and well-being, strengthening the voice of children and advocating for their health, safety, and education. ARC works with children in shelters, promotes online and offline safety, supports children with disabilities, and empowers girls. The organization developed the child-friendly educational storybook 'Boss of My Body' in partnership with UNICEF Maldives.

**Key Programs:**
- Corporate Ambassadorship Programme (since 2013)
- ARC AID programme for vulnerable children and families
- Child safety awareness (online and offline)
- Shelter support programs
- Can Do coalition for disability inclusion
- Publications and educational materials for children

### Maldivian Red Crescent (MRC)

**Registration:** Established by law  
**Focus Area:** Humanitarian, Disaster Management, Health, Youth  
**Website:** redcrescent.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** 10 branches and 20 units nationwide – the widest geographic reach of any CSO in the Maldives

The largest humanitarian organisation in the Maldives with over 4,440 volunteers in 10 branches and 20 units across the country. MRC focuses on Disaster Management, Health and well-being, and Youth empowerment. Over the past 13 years, MRC has carried out programs in Emergency Response, Disaster Preparedness, Epidemic preparedness, First Aid, Mental Health and Psychosocial Support (MHPSS), Patient Transport Services, Social Care and Inclusion, Promoting Healthy Living, and Youth empowerment.

**Key Programs:**
- Emergency response and disaster preparedness
- First aid services and training
- Mental Health and Psychosocial Support (MHPSS)
- Bed-to-bed patient transportation
- Epidemic control and response
- Social care and inclusion for migrants and vulnerable groups
- Youth empowerment programmes

### Care Society

**Registration:** Founded 9 November 1998  
**Focus Area:** Disability Services, Community-Based Rehabilitation  
**Phone:** 3316072  
**Email:** care88epss@gmail.com  
**Website:** caresociety.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'; CBR programmes in S. Addu, GDh. Thinadhoo, V. Keyodhoo/Felidhoo, HDh. Hanimaadhoo, HA. Maarandhoo

The largest non-profit organization in the Maldives dedicated to serving persons with disabilities. Since 2004, Care has implemented community-based rehabilitation (CBR) programs. In collaboration with Handicap International, CBR centres were established in Addu (Hithadhoo) and GDh. (Thinadhoo). With FIDA International, CBR was extended to V. Keyodhoo, V. Felidhoo, HDh. Hanimaadhoo, and HA. Maarandhoo. The Care Development Center (CDC), established in 2010, caters for autism, hearing/speech impairment, cerebral palsy, Down syndrome, learning disability, and intellectual disability.

**Key Programs:**
- Care Development Center (CDC) for children with disabilities
- Community-Based Rehabilitation (CBR) in outer atolls
- Advocacy and awareness campaigns for disability rights
- Support services for various disability types

**Phone:** 7709181  
**Email:** mapd.mv@outlook.com

### Blind and Visually Impaired Society of Maldives (BVISM)

**Registration:** Registered January 2010  
**Focus Area:** Visual Impairment, Advocacy, Skills Training  
**Phone:** 7553755  
**Email:** bvism2010@gmail.com  
**Website:** bvism.org  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'

BVISM advocates for the rights and wellbeing of the blind community in the Maldives. Programs include annual camps with physical exercise, blind football (first in Maldives), blood donation events, life skills sessions, fire awareness, disaster management, and first aid training. BVISM also initiated the first complete Quran recording by a Maldivian (December 2020).

**Key Programs:**
- Annual camps with life skills and awareness training
- Blind football introduction
- Quran recording project
- Advocacy for accessibility and inclusion
- Collaboration with government on disability policy

### Maldives Deaf Association (MDA)

**Registration:** Established 11 September 2008  
**Focus Area:** Deaf Rights, Sign Language, Inclusion  
**Phone:** 7601211

MDA has been empowering the deaf community in the Maldives since 2008. MDA raises awareness on challenges faced by persons who are deaf and hosts inclusive community events.

**Key Programs:**

### Transparency Maldives

**Focus Area:** Anti-Corruption, Good Governance, Accountability  
**Phone:** 3304017  
**Email:** office@transparencymaldives.org  
**Website:** transparency.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'; national advocacy scope

National Chapter of Transparency International. TM promotes collaboration, awareness, and undertakes initiatives to improve governance and eliminate corruption. The organization views corruption as systemic and advocates for institutional reforms. TM has conducted anti-corruption trainings for cabinet ministers, state ministers, deputy ministers, and heads of institutions. TM also worked on the Climate Integrity Project and supported development of the Whistleblower Protection Act (2019).

**Key Programs:**
- Anti-corruption and good governance training for government officials
- Climate Integrity Project
- Whistleblower protection advocacy
- BAARU Project
- Institutional reform advocacy

### Maldivian Thalassaemia Society (MTS)

**Registration:** Founded 1994  
**Focus Area:** Thalassemia Support and Advocacy  
**Email:** maldivianthalsociety@gmail.com  
**Website:** thalassaemia.org.mv  
**Geographic Reach:** Based in Male'; advocacy covers all atolls

Established in 1994 to improve and enhance the quality of life of thalassaemics in the Maldives. About 50% of Maldivian thalassemia patients – many of them children – receive blood transfusion treatment at the country's only specialized clinic in Male'. MTS works with Trans Maldivian Airways to provide free flights for thalassemia patients requiring treatment in Male'.

**Key Programs:**
- Patient support and advocacy
- Free flight coordination with Trans Maldivian Airways
- Awareness campaigns on thalassemia prevention
- Support for families of thalassemia patients

### Journey

**Focus Area:** Drug Rehabilitation, After-Care, Prevention  
**Geographic Reach:** RISE program conducted across 13 islands

Journey is a community-based NGO providing after-care and relapse prevention for recovering drug addicts – the first of its kind in the Maldives. Journey brought together international experts to create RISE, the first evidence-based drug prevention program in the Maldives, which had been conducted on 13 islands by 2020. Note: Journey does not appear in the CSO registry under this name; it may be registered under a different formal name.

**Key Programs:**
- After-care and relapse prevention for recovering addicts
- RISE evidence-based prevention program (conducted on 13 islands)
- Community-based support for recovery

### Category Gaps

Several critical social service categories have very few CSOs:

- **Elderly/Aging:** Only 5 CSOs across the entire country mention elderly or senior citizens in their name – a severe gap given the Maldives' aging population challenge on outer islands
- **Disability:** Only 10 CSOs specifically reference disability, blindness, deafness, or autism – and nearly all are Male'-based
- **Drug/Substance abuse:** Only 9 CSOs reference drugs, rehabilitation, or recovery – despite substance abuse being one of the country's most pressing social challenges
- **Health (non-government):** 63 health-related CSOs, but many are professional associations rather than service delivery organizations

### Geographic Concentration

The overwhelming majority of national-level social CSOs are headquartered in Male'. While some conduct outreach programs (notably the Maldivian Red Crescent with 10 branches and 20 units, and Care Society's CBR programs in outer atolls), most outer island CSOs are small community development or youth associations with limited capacity for specialized social service delivery.

## 4. Recommendations for Program Coordination

### By Social Service Domain

#### Child Protection

- Advocating the Rights of Children (ARC) – lead child rights advocacy CSO; operates Corporate Ambassadorship Programme and shelter support
- Maldivian Red Crescent – youth empowerment and psychosocial support through 10 branches nationwide
- Local youth development associations on each island (340+ CSOs with youth/child focus)

#### Gender-Based Violence (GBV)

- Hope for Women – primary GBV advocacy organization
- Family Legal Clinic – pro bono legal aid for domestic violence and family law cases
- Women & Democracy – political and economic empowerment training in all 20 atolls
- Women on Boards – economic empowerment and board representation advocacy
- Local women's associations (54 CSOs with women/gender focus across atolls)

#### Disability Services

- Care Society – largest disability CSO; runs Care Development Center and Community-Based Rehabilitation in outer atolls
- Maldives Association of Persons with Disabilities (MAPD) – national cross-disability organization; vocational training and medical camps
- Blind and Visually Impaired Society of Maldives (BVISM) – visual impairment advocacy and skills training
- Maldives Deaf Association (MDA) – deaf rights and CRPD implementation
- Maldives Autism Association (MAA) – therapy and intervention for children aged 2-12
- Maldives Disabled Federation (MDF) – umbrella federation

#### Mental Health and Psychosocial Support

- Society for Health Education (SHE) – counselling, psychosocial services, and play therapy
- Mental Health Support Group (MHSG) – peer-support meetings for mental health conditions
- Hope & Support Society – free support groups and mental health workshops
- Mental Health Awareness Foundation (MHAF) – awareness and stigma reduction
- Maldivian Red Crescent – MHPSS services through branch network

#### Drug Prevention and Rehabilitation

- Journey – after-care, relapse prevention, and the RISE evidence-based prevention program (13 islands)
- Drug Prevention and Rehabilitation Alliance – prevention and rehab support
- Family Association for Anti-Narcotics and Self Support – family support for addiction
- Organization for Youth Against Drugs and Violence – youth-focused prevention
- Maldives Youth Rehabilitation Action (MAIRAA) – youth rehabilitation

#### Health (Thalassemia, Cancer, Other)

- Society for Health Education (SHE) – only thalassemia DNA testing facility in the country
- Maldivian Thalassaemia Society – patient support and free flight coordination
- Cancer Society of Maldives – cancer awareness and patient support
- Addu Thalassemia Society – atoll-level thalassemia support in Seenu Atoll
- Kidney Care Maldives – kidney disease support

#### Community Development and Resilience

- Island Development Associations (363 CSOs with development/community focus) – present on most inhabited islands
- Environmental CSOs (127 organizations) – climate resilience and environmental protection
- Maldivian Red Crescent – disaster preparedness and emergency response nationwide

#### Elderly Care

- Very few dedicated CSOs exist for elderly care – this is a critical gap
- Island development associations and welfare clubs may provide informal elderly support
- Government Social Protection programmes (elderly allowance, Fiyavathi) remain the primary mechanism

#### Good Governance and Accountability

- Transparency Maldives – anti-corruption, governance training, whistleblower protection
- Human Rights and Civil Justice Network – rights monitoring
- Maldives Centre for Human Rights and Democracy – human rights advocacy

### Strategic Recommendations

1. **Leverage the Maldivian Red Crescent's branch network** as the primary community-level delivery partner for social programs. With 10 branches and 20 units, MRC has the widest geographic reach of any CSO and trained volunteer capacity in first aid, MHPSS, and emergency response.

2. **Build capacity of island-level youth and development associations** to serve as frontline social service partners. Over 700 CSOs exist in outer islands under these categories, but most lack specialized social service training. A standardized "Community Social Worker" training module delivered through existing CSOs could rapidly expand local capacity.

3. **Address the elderly care CSO gap** urgently. With only 5 CSOs nationally referencing elderly issues, and the outer island population skewing increasingly older as youth migrate to Male', community-based elderly care CSOs need to be catalyzed – potentially through existing island development associations.

4. **Expand Care Society's Community-Based Rehabilitation model** to additional atolls. The CBR model (operational in Addu, GDh, Vaavu, HDh, HA) provides a proven framework for disability services in outer islands and could be replicated through government-CSO partnership agreements.

5. **Scale Journey's RISE drug prevention program** beyond the current 13 islands. As a tested, evidence-based prevention program, RISE could be delivered through partnerships with island youth associations in high-risk communities.

6. **Create formal "CSO partnership registers"** at each Family and Children's Service Centre (FCSC), documenting which local CSOs can be mobilized for case referrals, community awareness, and client support in each atoll.
