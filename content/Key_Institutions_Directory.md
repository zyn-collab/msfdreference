# Directory of Key Institutions and Agencies

This directory provides essential information on the principal institutions, agencies, and organisations involved in social policy formulation, implementation, and oversight in the Maldives.

### Government Ministries and Departments

**Ministry of Social and Family Development (MoSFD)**
- *Previously:* Ministry of Gender, Family and Social Services (MoGFSS, 2018-2023); Ministry of Gender and Family (2013-2018); Ministry of Health and Family (2012-2013)
- *Mandate:* Policy formulation and service delivery for child protection and welfare, gender equality and GBV protection, social welfare for vulnerable groups (elderly, persons with disabilities), and community-level social service coordination
- *Key units:* Child and Family Protection Services (CFPS); Policy Department; Social Protection and Services Division; Gender Equality Division
- *Parent body:* Government of Maldives (executive branch)

**Ministry of Education (MoE)**
- *Mandate:* Pre-primary, primary, and secondary education policy; curriculum development; teacher management; school administration
- *Key units:* Policy Planning and Research Division; Quality Assurance Department (QAD); National Institute of Education (NIE)

**Ministry of Higher Education (MoHE)**
- *Mandate:* Tertiary education oversight; Maldives Qualifications Authority (MQA); higher education financing (First Degree Scheme, loan programmes); TVET coordination; Maldives National Skills Development Authority (MNSDA)

**Ministry of Health (MoH)**
- *Mandate:* Health policy, regulation, public health protection, and health service delivery oversight
- *Key instruments:* Health Master Plan 2016-2025; National Mental Health Policy 2015-2025

**Ministry of Home Affairs**
- *Mandate:* Internal security, prison administration, civil registration
- *Relevant functions:* Oversight of correctional system (Maafushi Prison), co-implementation of NAP P/CVE

**Ministry of Islamic Affairs**
- *Mandate:* Religious guidance, mosque administration, fatwa issuance, hajj coordination
- *Relevance:* Interpretation of Islamic law as it intersects with family law, FGM, sexual offences, and social norms

### Statutory Bodies and Agencies

**National Social Protection Agency (NSPA)**
- *Established:* 2008 (initially as department; later upgraded)
- *Mandate:* Central coordinating and implementing agency for social assistance programmes
- *Programmes administered:* Aasandha (via Aasandha Company Ltd); disability allowance; single parent allowance; foster parent allowance; food assistance
- *Key system:* Social Protection Information System (SPIS); developing Single Registry of poor/vulnerable households with World Bank support

**Maldives Pension Administration Office (MPAO)**
- *Established:* Under Pension Act (Act 8/2009)
- *Mandate:* Independent statutory body administering Old-Age Basic Pension (MVR 5,000/month for citizens 65+) and Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme (MRPS -- defined contribution)
- *Assets under management:* Over MVR 15 billion (2023)

**Child and Family Protection Services (CFPS)**
- *Parent body:* MoSFD
- *Mandate:* Operational child protection casework at national level. Manages all 20 atoll-level FCSCs. Specialised teams for assessment, investigation, legal liaison, case coordination
- *Data system:* Maldives Child Protection Database (MCPD, launched 2016)
- *Helpline:* 1412 (toll-free, 24-hour)

**Family and Children Service Centres (FCSCs)**
- *Number:* 20 (one per administrative atoll)
- *Managed by:* CFPS centrally; staffed locally
- *Functions:* Frontline social service delivery for child protection, DV, elderly welfare, disability services, family disputes
- *Typical staffing:* 2-5 social workers per centre

**Family Protection Authority (FPA)**
- *Established:* Under DVPA 2012
- *Mandate:* Central coordinating body for DV response: receives/responds to DV reports, coordinates multi-agency response, maintains DV database, conducts public awareness, monitors compliance, advises government
- *Reporting:* DV case data (19 cases in 2013; 642 in 2016)

**National Drug Agency (NDA)**
- *Established:* Under Drugs Act (Act 17/2011), replacing Narcotics Control Board
- *Mandate:* Drug prevention, treatment, rehabilitation, data collection, treatment standards, laboratory services
- *Facilities:* 10 treatment facilities established (7 operational as of 2021)

**Human Rights Commission of the Maldives (HRCM)**
- *Mandate:* Independent statutory body; promote and protect human rights. Investigates complaints, inspects detention/care facilities, submits shadow reports to UN treaty bodies
- *Key publications:* "Rights Side of Life" surveys (2005, 2011, 2019); CRPD Shadow Report (2023); CEDAW submission (2021); disability baseline assessment (2010)
- *Website:* https://www.hrcm.org.mv

**Children's Ombudsperson**
- *Established:* 2020 (under CRPA 2019)
- *Mandate:* Independent office: investigate complaints, monitor CRPA compliance, make recommendations to government and Majlis, conduct child rights audits, convene inter-agency forums
- *Key event:* Child Rights Symposium 2022 (co-hosted with UNICEF)

**Council for the Protection of the Rights of the Child**
- *Established:* Under CRPA 2019
- *Mandate:* Multi-sectoral coordinating body appointed by the President; policy advisory role on child protection

**Disability Council**
- *Established:* Under Disability Act 2010
- *Mandate:* Monitoring body under ministry responsible for disability; proposes recommendations, resolves issues
- *Critique:* Only one member with a disability elected to represent entire disability community

**Maldives Qualifications Authority (MQA)**
- *Mandate:* Quality assurance for higher education and TVET; maintains Maldives National Qualifications Framework (MNQF, 10 levels)

**Maldives Bureau of Statistics (MBS)**
- *Mandate:* National statistical agency. Administers Census (2014, 2022), HIES, and other national surveys
- *Key data:* Population and Housing Census 2022; HIES 2019/20

**Local Government Authority (LGA)**
- *Established:* Under Decentralisation Act 2010
- *Mandate:* Oversight of elected atoll and island councils. Coordination with CFPS and ministry on community-level social service delivery. Manages IBAMA project implementation channel.

**Women's Development Committees (WDCs)**
- *Mandate:* Women's representation at island level. Established in each island council area
- *Representation:* Local council women's seats reached 39.7% after 33% quota introduced
- *Challenges:* Effectiveness varies enormously across islands; many WDC members unaware of their legal mandate

### Judicial Institutions

**Family Court**
- *Jurisdiction:* Marriage, divorce, custody, maintenance, prenuptial agreements
- *Location:* Male (primary); limited access in atolls
- *Key issue:* MVR 2 million in unclaimed maintenance payments accumulated

**Juvenile Court**
- *Established:* 1997
- *Jurisdiction:* Criminal cases involving persons under 18
- *Key issue:* No separate juvenile detention facilities established; flogging not abolished for juveniles

**Drug Court**
- *Established:* Under Drugs Act 2011
- *Mandate:* Specialised court for drug use cases; diversion toward treatment/rehabilitation rather than incarceration

**Prosecutor General's Office (PGO)**
- *Mandate:* Criminal prosecution. Only 3% of DV/GBV cases reported to police are submitted for prosecution. Conviction rate for those reaching court: 16.7% (2016-2019).

**Attorney General's Office (AGO)**
- *Mandate:* Chief legal advisor to the government; legislative drafting; international treaty compliance

### Higher Education Institutions

**Maldives National University (MNU)**
- *Established:* 2011 (upgraded from MCHE, est. 1998)
- *Key programmes:* BSW (Faculty of Health Sciences -- only professional social work programme); Faculty of Education; Faculty of Health Sciences; multiple other faculties
- *Role:* Primary domestic institution for social work training

**Islamic University of Maldives (IUM)**
- *Mandate:* Islamic studies, Sharia law, Arabic language, education
- *Relevance:* Produces graduates whose jurisprudential training shapes family court practice, religious guidance on social issues

**TVET Institutions**
- *Maldives National Skills Development Authority (MNSDA):* Regulates all TVET programmes
- *Maldives Polytechnic:* Primary public TVET institution
- *Private colleges:* 12 private higher education institutions operating alongside MNU and IUM

### Key Non-Governmental Organisations

**Advocating the Rights of Children (ARC)**
- *Focus:* Child rights advocacy, child protection policy engagement

**Care Society**
- *Focus:* Mental health, substance abuse, child rights. Co-authored NGO Shadow Report on CRC (2006)

**Society for Health Education (SHE)**
- *Focus:* Health education and promotion

**Transparency Maldives**
- *Focus:* Anti-corruption, governance accountability, research on corruption vulnerabilities in health and social sectors

**Family Legal Clinic**
- *Focus:* Free legal assistance to women on family law, custody, maintenance, DV cases. Joint reports to UN bodies on GBV

**Hope for Women**
- *Focus:* Women's rights, GBV response, legal advocacy. Joint submissions to CEDAW and Working Group on Discrimination Against Women and Girls

**Hama Jamiyya**
- *Focus:* Child rights NGO. Co-authored NGO Shadow Report on CRC (2006)

**Journey**
- *Focus:* Drug rehabilitation and prevention. Conducted 2006 Rapid Assessment (28% of respondents reported injection drug use)

**AgedCare Maldives**
- *Focus:* Elderly welfare, advocacy for ageing population services

**Maldives Thalassaemia Association**
- *Focus:* Thalassaemia awareness, patient support, advocacy for prevention programmes

**Diabetes Society of Maldives**
- *Focus:* Diabetes awareness, NCD prevention

**Equality Now**
- *Focus:* International women's rights organisation; joint reporting on Maldivian GBV legal framework to UN bodies

**Musawah**
- *Focus:* Global movement for equality and justice in the Muslim family. Applied analytical framework to Maldivian family law, demonstrating jurisprudential basis for reform within Islamic principles

### International Partners

**UNICEF Maldives**
- *Role:* Technical assistance for child protection (CRPA development, IBAMA, MCPD), education, adolescent development. UNICEF Situation Analysis 2021 (updated 2025). Significant programme expansion post-2004 tsunami.

**UNDP Maldives**
- *Role:* Governance, human rights, climate adaptation, P/CVE. Partner on "Rights Side of Life" surveys, NAP P/CVE 2020-2024, Human Development Reports.

**UNFPA Maldives**
- *Role:* Gender equality, GBV prevention, SRH, population data. Commissioned Fulu (2007) study, GBV-during-COVID analysis, McCabe (2023) legal review. "Rights Side of Life" survey partner.

**WHO Maldives**
- *Role:* Health systems strengthening, NCD prevention, mental health (mhGAP), communicable disease control. Country Cooperation Strategies (2013-2017 and successors).

**World Bank**
- *Role:* Poverty assessment, social protection reform, social registry pilot, Systematic Country Diagnostic. Drug rehabilitation evaluation (2010). NSPF 2022-2026 technical assistance.

**Asian Development Bank (ADB)**
- *Role:* Economic development, social protection analysis, gender assessment, climate economics. SPI study (2012). ADB (2025) gender and development data.

**International Labour Organization (ILO)**
- *Role:* Employment standards, forced labour prevention. Maldives has ratified all eight fundamental ILO Conventions.

**UNODC**
- *Role:* Drug abuse surveys, drug control technical assistance, counter-narcotics capacity building. Supported 2003 RSA, 2008 BBS, and country project 2010.
