## National Ageing Policy – 2025 Q2

**Lead:** Shifaza – ERW | **Source:** Social Council Paper

**Related mandates:** (1) Establishing protection policies for women, children, elderly persons, persons with disabilities, and individuals requiring state protection and care. (2) Implementing protection policies for women, elderly persons, persons with disabilities, and individuals requiring state protection and care, including securing necessary resources for policy implementation and monitoring these activities. (21) Identifying and addressing emerging issues affecting the rights of women, children, elderly persons, persons with disabilities, individuals requiring state protection and care, and their families, working with relevant sectors to resolve these matters. (22) Developing and implementing national policies for strengthening family relationships and building healthy families in collaboration with relevant parties.

**Description:** This project involves the finalization, endorsement, and launch of the National Ageing Policy. The process includes developing a Social Council Paper to facilitate its formal adoption. The policy aims to align with international standards, such as the Madrid International Plan of Action on Ageing, and integrate existing national health and social policies into a coordinated framework for the elderly population.

**Human impact/purpose:** With the Maldives set to experience rapid population ageing in upcoming decades, the purpose of this policy is to create a secure and supportive environment where older persons are guaranteed rights to health, dignity, and responsible living. It seeks to address the challenges of a rapidly ageing society by focusing on development, protection, lifelong learning, optimal health, enabling environments, and income security for the elderly.

## National Elderly Action Plan – 2025 Q2

**Lead:** Shifaza – ERW | **Source:** Ageing Policy

**Related mandates:** (2) Implementing protection policies for women, elderly persons, persons with disabilities, and individuals requiring state protection and care, including securing necessary resources for policy implementation and monitoring these activities. (42) Developing, implementing, and monitoring development projects related to the ministry's areas in alignment with the National Development Plan, in collaboration with relevant parties.

**Description:** This project focuses on creating a tangible 2-year action plan to operationalize the National Ageing Policy. The development will be done in collaboration with technical support from UNESCAP, followed by a process of stakeholder consultation, validation, and official endorsement.

**Human impact/purpose:** The purpose is to translate the broad goals of the National Ageing Policy into concrete, actionable steps with clear responsibilities and timelines, ensuring the policy's vision of a supportive environment for the elderly is effectively implemented across multiple sectors.

## Ufaaveri Raasthaa – 2025 Q4

**Lead:** Mizufa – ERW | **Source:** [VM.23-22.4.3], Riyaasee Bayaan 2025

**Related mandates:** (K)(2) Plan and implement sports, recreational activities, and programs for senior citizen participation. (K)(3) Plan and implement programs to promote good health among senior citizens, prepare them for healthy aging, prevent diseases, and improve overall wellbeing. (32) Developing skills and capabilities necessary for protecting the rights of women, children, elderly persons, persons with disabilities, and individuals requiring state protection and care, establishing gender equality, improving family relationships, and promoting healthy child upbringing, while raising community awareness on these matters.

**Description:** This project is designed to develop and implement continuous, island-level programs that promote healthy and dignified ageing. Key activities include creating and distributing educational toolkits titled 'Healthy Ageing/Ageing with Dignity', and establishing an activity calendar to roll out these programs across islands.

**Human impact/purpose:** The project aims to directly empower elderly individuals with knowledge and practical tools to improve their quality of life. By providing accessible resources and structured activities, it seeks to enhance physical and mental wellness and foster a culture of dignity for the ageing population.

## Digital Literacy Program for Elderly – 2025 Q3

**Lead:** Shifaza – ERW | **Source:** Social Council Paper

**Description:** This project involves the design and nationwide rollout of a Digital Literacy Program specifically for the elderly. The process includes consultations with the Maldives Pension Administration Office (MPAO) and other stakeholders, program design, development of a Social Council Paper for endorsement, and phased implementation starting with elderly community centres ('Israhvehinge Naadhees') in the Male' area before expanding to the atolls.

**Human impact/purpose:** The purpose is to empower older persons with essential modern skills, enabling them to navigate digital services effectively. This enhances their independence, connectivity, and security in an increasingly digital world.

## Community Based Elderly Care (CBEC) Program – 2025 Q4

**Lead:** Rishmee – ERW | **Source:** Riyaasee Bayaan 2025

**Related mandates:** (31) Implementing social protection programs in collaboration with government departments and civil society organizations established at atoll and island levels.

**Description:** This project aims to develop and implement a Community-Based Elderly Care (CBEC) program across the nation. Key tasks include training community-level members and "Trainers of Trainers" (TOTs) on elderly care, incorporating the 'Rehapp' mobile application to build capacity, and rolling out the program in five atolls. It also involves establishing a monitoring mechanism and a referral and feedback system for social protection services.

**Human impact/purpose:** The project's goal is to strengthen the capacity of local communities to provide effective care for the elderly, enabling them to age in place. By training local stakeholders and establishing a structured, decentralized care system, it enhances the support network for older persons and improves access to services.

## Strategy for Long Term Care – 2025 Q4

**Lead:** Shifaza – ERW | **Source:** SGII Project

**Related mandates:** (1) Establishing protection policies for women, children, elderly persons, persons with disabilities, and individuals requiring state protection and care. (36) Conducting and authorizing research related to the ministry's areas of work, maintaining research quality and ethics, and promoting a research environment in this field.

**Description:** This project focuses on developing and subsequently implementing a national Strategy for Long Term Care for the elderly. The development phase will be conducted with technical support from the Asian Development Bank's (ADB) SGII Project.

**Human impact/purpose:** The purpose is to create a structured, sustainable framework for providing long-term care to a growing elderly population, many of whom will face non-communicable diseases and frailty. This strategy will address the increasing need for integrated health and social care services, ensuring that older persons can receive continuous support as their health needs evolve.
