# Glossary of Maldivian Social Policy Terms

This glossary defines key Dhivehi, Arabic, and English terms used throughout the encyclopedia, with brief contextual notes on their usage in Maldivian social policy discourse.

### A

**Aasandha** -- The national universal health insurance scheme, launched in 2012 under the National Social Health Insurance Act (Act 15/2011). Covers all Maldivian citizens from birth with no premiums, co-payments, or deductibles. "Husnuvaa Aasandha" (2014) removed the annual benefit cap, making coverage unlimited.

**Atoll** -- A ring-shaped coral reef formation enclosing a lagoon, from the Dhivehi word *atholhu*. The Maldives comprises 26 natural atolls organised into 20 administrative atolls for governance purposes.

### B

**Beys (Dhivehi beys)** -- Traditional Maldivian medicine. Practitioners (*hakeem*) use herbal remedies, religious incantations, and traditional healing methods. Still practised alongside modern medicine, particularly on outer islands.

### C

**CFPS** -- Child and Family Protection Services. The operational unit within MoSFD responsible for all child protection casework at the national level. Manages all 20 atoll-level FCSCs. Successor to the Unit for the Rights of Children (URC, est. 1992).

**CSG** -- Community Social Group. Volunteer community-level groups initiated in 2010 and revived under the IBAMA project to extend child protection and social service awareness into communities where FCSCs do not have a direct presence.

### D

**Drug Court** -- Specialised court established under the Drugs Act 2011 to handle substance use cases with an explicit aim of diverting drug-dependent persons toward treatment and rehabilitation rather than incarceration.

### E

**Edhuruge** -- Traditional home-based Maldivian schools, typically run by a learned community member, focused on Quran recitation, basic Arabic literacy, and rudimentary arithmetic. The primary form of education for centuries before modern schooling. Singular: *edhuruge*; the system operated at the island level with no standardised curriculum.

### F

**Fandiyaaru** -- A judge or magistrate in the Maldivian judicial system. Historically, the *fandiyaaru* administered Islamic law at the island level, adjudicating family disputes, inheritance matters, and criminal cases.

**FCSC** -- Family and Children Service Centre. Frontline social service delivery points, with one in each of the 20 administrative atolls. Staffed by 2-5 social workers handling child protection, DV, elderly welfare, disability, and family disputes.

**FGM/C** -- Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting. Affects 13% of Maldivian women aged 15-49 (DHS 2016-17). Not criminalised in the Maldives. Some religious leaders endorse it as *sunnah* (recommended practice).

**Fiqh** -- Islamic jurisprudence; the body of scholarly interpretation of Islamic law derived from the Quran and Sunnah. In the Maldives, *fiqh* principles inform family law (marriage, divorce, custody, inheritance), criminal law (hadd punishments), and social norms.

**FPA** -- Family Protection Authority. The central coordinating body for domestic violence response, created under the DVPA 2012.

### G

**GEAP** -- Gender Equality Action Plan. The operational plan for implementing the Gender Equality Act 2016. GEAP 2022-2026 launched March 2022 with a Steering Committee chaired by the President.

**Geveshi Gulhun** -- Literally "connected at home" or "home connection." A national awareness campaign launched during COVID-19 to combat domestic violence during lockdowns, encouraging reporting and providing information on support services.

### H

**Haalubelun** -- Literally "health check" or "condition assessment." Used in the context of health assessments, particularly for elderly home visit programmes and disability evaluations.

**HRCM** -- Human Rights Commission of the Maldives. Independent statutory body mandated to promote and protect human rights; investigates complaints, inspects institutions, reports to UN treaty bodies.

**Hulhumale** -- Reclaimed island adjacent to Male, developed from 1997 as a satellite settlement to address the housing crisis. Phase I completed 2003; Phase II under development. Population exceeds 40,000.

**Husnuvaa** -- Literally "beautiful life" or "good living." Used in two contexts: (1) *Husnuvaa Aasandha* -- the unlimited health insurance scheme introduced in 2014; (2) *Husnuvaa* social housing programmes.

### I

**IBAMA** -- "You and I Together" (*Ib* + *ama*). A community-level child and family protection project developed by MoGFSS and UNICEF, managed through the LGA via atoll and island councils. Provides social protection and statutory services at the island level through volunteer community groups.

**IGMH** -- Indira Gandhi Memorial Hospital. The national referral hospital and primary tertiary care facility in Male. Houses the National Centre for Mental Health (est. 2019).

### J

**Jinni** -- Spirits or supernatural beings in Islamic cosmology. Belief in *jinni* possession as a cause of mental illness is widespread in the Maldives and contributes to stigma against help-seeking for psychiatric conditions.

### K

**Kadheeb** -- Traditional island chief, historically appointed by the sultan (later by the president). The *kadheeb* served as local administrator, dispute resolver, and overseer of community affairs. Replaced by elected island councillors under the Decentralisation Act 2010.

**Kilegefaanu** -- Noble families in traditional Maldivian social hierarchy. Alongside *beys* (lords), they constituted the upper social strata with marriage and status privileges.

**Kuda kudhin** -- Literally "small children." Used colloquially and in policy discourse to refer to young children, particularly in the context of early childhood care, child protection, and educational provisions.

### L

**LGA** -- Local Government Authority. Oversees elected atoll and island councils under the Decentralisation Act 2010.

### M

**Madhana** -- The social health insurance scheme introduced in 2008, covering civil servants, pensioners, senior citizens, persons below the poverty line, and persons with disabilities. Predecessor to Aasandha. Coverage up to MVR 2,000/year.

**Madrasa** -- Islamic school providing religious education. In the Maldivian context, *madrasa* refers to both traditional religious learning institutions and modern Arabic-medium schools that combine Islamic studies with secular subjects. Distinguished from the older *edhuruge* system by more formalised curricula.

**Mahram** -- A close male blood relative with whom marriage is permanently prohibited under Islamic law (father, brother, son, uncle, nephew, etc.). Under the Family Act, a mother loses priority custody of her child if she marries a *non-mahram* man.

**Mas hiki** -- Fish drying and processing, traditionally performed by women on outer islands. A key component of women's economic participation in the traditional Maldivian economy.

**MBS** -- Maldives Bureau of Statistics. National statistical agency responsible for the Census, HIES, and other national surveys.

**MCPD** -- Maldives Child Protection Database. Digital case management system launched 2016, linking all FCSCs with central CFPS.

**Merana** -- Aasandha overseas treatment programme (introduced 2019). Covers treatment at partner hospitals in India, Sri Lanka, and Malaysia for conditions not treatable domestically.

**MNU** -- Maldives National University. Established 2011; primary domestic institution for higher education including the only professional social work degree programme.

**MoSFD** -- Ministry of Social and Family Development (current name under the Muizzu administration, 2023-present).

**MPAO** -- Maldives Pension Administration Office. Administers the Old-Age Basic Pension and MRPS.

**MQA** -- Maldives Qualifications Authority. Quality assurance for higher education and TVET; maintains the MNQF.

**MRPS** -- Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme. Mandatory defined-contribution pension for all employees, administered by MPAO.

**Mu'amalat** -- Social and contractual relations in Islamic jurisprudence, as distinct from *ibadat* (fixed devotional obligations). Reform advocates argue that family law falls within *mu'amalat* and is therefore open to rational reconsideration and contextual reinterpretation.

### N

**NDA** -- National Drug Agency. Established under the Drugs Act 2011 as the primary body for drug prevention, treatment, rehabilitation, and data collection.

**NDR** -- National Disability Registry. Maintained by NSPA/MoSFD. Contains 13,656 registrants (December 2024) against a Census count of 24,401 persons with disabilities.

**NEET** -- Not in Education, Employment, or Training. A key indicator of youth vulnerability. Young women's NEET rate in the atolls reaches 42.7%.

**NSPA** -- National Social Protection Agency. Central coordinating body for social assistance programmes: Aasandha, disability allowance, single parent allowance, foster parent allowance, food assistance.

**Nushuz** -- "Disobedience" or "recalcitrance" in Islamic family law. A wife deemed *nushuz* may forfeit her right to maintenance under traditional *fiqh* as applied in Maldivian courts.

### O

**OneGov** -- The government's digital integration platform allowing different social services to be accessed through a single portal. Zakat House services integrated in late 2024.

### P

**P/CVE** -- Preventing and Countering Violent Extremism. The national policy approach articulated in the NAP P/CVE 2020-2024, combining security measures with social interventions.

### Q

**Qiwamah** -- The Islamic concept of male guardianship or authority over women, derived from Quran 4:34. Maldivian family law embeds *qiwamah* principles in marriage guardianship, custody, and divorce provisions.

### R

**Raivaru** -- A traditional social stratification system in the Maldives, sometimes described as caste-like. Influenced marriage patterns and social standing, though less rigid than South Asian caste systems.

### S

**SAP** -- Strategic Action Plan. The government-wide strategic planning instrument. SAP 2019-2023 was the most recent completed cycle.

**Satr** -- The Islamic principle of concealing others' faults or private matters. In child protection and GBV contexts, *satr* operates as a cultural barrier to reporting abuse, reinforcing family privacy norms that suppress disclosure.

**Sihr** -- Sorcery or black magic in Islamic cosmology. Attribution of mental illness to *sihr* contributes to stigma and delayed help-seeking for psychiatric conditions in the Maldives.

**SPIS** -- Social Protection Information System. NSPA's database holding records of all individuals receiving pensions, allowances, and subsidies. Linked to the national ID database.

### T

**Taghut** -- An Arabic term meaning illegitimate authority or tyranny. Used by extremist groups in Maldivian prisons to delegitimise the government and frame their activities as jihad against an un-Islamic state.

**Tahlil marriage** -- A temporary or "chain-breaking" marriage. Under the three-consecutive-divorce rule in the Family Act, a woman who has been divorced three times by the same husband cannot remarry him unless she first contracts and consummates a marriage with another man. *Tahlil* marriages, sometimes arranged for payment, instrumentalise women's marital status.

### U

**Urf** -- Customary practice. Historically, social disputes in the Maldives were resolved through a combination of Islamic jurisprudence (*fiqh*) and customary practice (*urf*) at the island level.

### W

**Wali / Wali veriyaa** -- Male guardian. Under the Family Act, a woman requires a male guardian's consent for marriage. The guardian hierarchy follows Islamic jurisprudence: father, paternal grandfather, brother, paternal uncle, etc. The father is also the principal judicial guardian (*wali*) of a child.

**WDC** -- Women's Development Committee. Island-level committees for women's representation, established in each island council area.

**Wilayah** -- The broader Islamic concept of male authority or guardianship, encompassing both family and public dimensions. Together with *qiwamah*, the Musawah framework analysis identifies *wilayah* as the structural basis of gender asymmetry in Maldivian family law.

### Z

**Zakat** -- Obligatory almsgiving in Islam, one of the five pillars. In the Maldives, *Zakat al-Mal* and *Zakat al-Fitr* are collected and distributed through the Maldives Zakat House, funding support for the poor, medical assistance, and housing for destitute families. Historically, zakat was the primary institutionalised form of wealth redistribution, collected and distributed locally through island chiefs and mosque committees.

**Zina** -- Extramarital sexual intercourse, a criminal offence under the Maldivian Penal Code (Section 411(d): 100 lashes). The *zina* framework creates particular risks for women and girls who become pregnant through rape, as pregnancy provides visible evidence. An estimated 85% of persons sentenced to flogging under *zina* provisions are women and girls. Child victims of sexual exploitation aged 13+ may face prosecution for *zina* rather than being treated as victims.
