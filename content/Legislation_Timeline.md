# Chronological Timeline of Major Social Legislation

This appendix provides a comprehensive timeline of legislation, policies, treaty ratifications, and institutional milestones referenced across the encyclopedia. The table is organised chronologically, with each entry identifying the year, the legislation or policy instrument, its key provisions, and the sector(s) to which it principally relates.

### Pre-Independence and Early Republic

| Year | Legislation / Policy / Milestone | Key Provisions | Sector |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------|--------|
| 1887 | British Protectorate Agreement | Maldives retains internal self-governance; Britain manages external defence and foreign affairs. No social policy intervention by Britain. | Governance |
| 1927 | Establishment of Majeediyya School | First modern school in Male; English-medium instruction for boys only. | Education |
| 1944 | Establishment of Aminiya School | First formal educational institution for girls in Male; one of the earliest for women in the Indian Ocean region. | Education, Gender |
| 1952-1953 | First Republic (brief) | Sultanate briefly replaced by a republic; no significant social legislation. First republican constitution promulgated. | Governance |
| 1965 | Independence from Britain | Full sovereignty restored; Maldives joins the United Nations. | Governance |
| 1968 | Second Republic established | National referendum establishes the republic under President Ibrahim Nasir. Centralisation of governance in Male. | Governance |
| 1972 | First tourist resort opens (Kurumba) | Beginning of the tourism economy; triggers internal migration to Male and exposure to international influences. | Economy |
| 1974 | First Vocational Training Centre | Established in Male; first formal vocational education institution. | Education |

### The Gayoom Era (1978-2008)

| Year | Legislation / Policy / Milestone | Key Provisions | Sector |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------|--------|
| 1977 | Law No. 17/77 -- The Law on Drugs | First drug control legislation. Criminalises planting, production, import, export, selling, buying, and possession of illegal drugs. Life imprisonment for trafficking; 5-12 years for possession under 1 gram; automatic trafficking presumption for over 1 gram. | Substance Abuse, Criminal Justice |
| 1978 | Gayoom assumes presidency | 30-year presidency begins. Commitment to building primary schools on every inhabited island. Health centres established in all 20 atoll capitals (1978-1990). | Governance |
| 1979 | Islaahiya reform facility established | First institutional care facility; housed both delinquent youth and children in need of care without differentiation. | Child Protection |
| 1979 | First A-Level centre (Majeediyya School) | Higher secondary education for boys in Male. | Education |
| 1980 | First Health Sector Plan | Three-year medium-term plan following Alma-Ata Declaration (1978) primary health care principles. | Health |
| 1981 | Higher secondary education for girls (Aminiya) | A-Level instruction extended to girls. | Education, Gender |
| 1984 | CEDAW ratification | Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women ratified with reservations on Articles 7(a) (political participation -- presidency) and 16 (marriage and family law). | Gender, International Law |
| 1984 | Maldives certified malaria-free | First country in the South-East Asia WHO Region to achieve malaria elimination. | Health |
| 1984 | ICERD ratification | International Convention on the Elimination of All Racial Discrimination ratified (no reservations). | International Law |
| 1985 | First atoll-level hospital (Addu City) | Expansion of secondary health care beyond Male. | Health |
| 1987 | Tourism Training School established | Formalised training for the tourism sector workforce. | Education |
| 1987 | Gayoom UN General Assembly address | Landmark speech warning that rising seas could make the Maldives uninhabitable; establishes Maldives as a voice in international climate advocacy. | Climate |
| 1991 | CRC ratification | Convention on the Rights of the Child ratified on 11 February 1991 with reservations on Article 14(1) (freedom of religion) and Article 21 (adoption -- not permitted under Islamic law). | Child Protection, International Law |
| 1991 | Law on the Protection of the Rights of the Child (Law 9/91) | First child protection legislation. Largely declaratory; no enforcement mechanisms, no definitions of abuse, no mandatory reporting, no penalties. | Child Protection |
| 1992 | Unit for the Rights of Children (URC) established | First dedicated government child protection unit, initially within Ministry of Home Affairs. Secretariat for National Council for Children's Rights. | Child Protection |
| 1993 | URC transferred to Ministry of Youth, Women and Social Welfare | Shift from security-oriented to welfare-oriented institutional framing for children's issues. | Child Protection |
| 1993 | CEDAW officially acceded to | Formal accession (some sources cite 1984 signature, 1993 accession). Reservations on Articles 7(a) and 16. | Gender, International Law |
| 1995 | Law No. 17/77 amendment | Amendments to the original drug law. | Substance Abuse |
| 1996 | Health Master Plan 1996-2005 | First long-term health sector strategic plan. | Health |
| 1997 | Narcotics Control Board (NCB) established | First institutional coordination body for drug policy. Accompanied by Drug Rehabilitation Centre (DRC) on Himafushi island. | Substance Abuse |
| 1997 | Juvenile Court established | First specialised juvenile justice institution in the Maldives. | Child Protection, Criminal Justice |
| 1997 | First national A-Level examinations | Held at Centre for Higher Secondary Education (CHSE) in Male. | Education |
| 1997 | Hulhumale land reclamation begins | Major housing intervention; reclamation of land within the Hulhule/Farukolhufushi lagoon. First residents move in 2004. | Housing |
| 1998 | Maldives College of Higher Education (MCHE) established | Consolidation of multiple training centres into a single higher education institution. Precursor to MNU. | Education |
| 2000 | Family Act (Act No. 4/2000) | Primary law governing marriage, divorce, custody, and maintenance. Codifies Islamic family law principles. Establishes guardian requirements for women's marriage; permits polygamy; creates asymmetric divorce process; minimum child maintenance MVR 2,000/month. Amended 2013 (Act 11/2013) and 2016 (Act 9/2016). | Family Law, Gender |
| 2000 | Maldives Accreditation Board (MAB) created | First post-secondary education regulatory body. | Education |
| 2001 | Family Regulation of 2001 | Implementing regulation for the Family Act, promulgated under Section 72 of the Act. | Family Law |
| 2001 | Repeal of Penal Code Chapter 3, Section 72 | Removed mandatory reporting provisions from the old Penal Code, creating a gap in child abuse reporting until CRPA 2019. | Child Protection, Criminal Justice |
| 2002 | Land Law of Maldives | Introduced condominium registration (strata titling), allowing individual unit registration and mortgaging within buildings. | Housing |
| 2003 | First National Drug Use Survey (Rapid Situation Assessment) | UNODC-supported study: 3,909 individuals assessed. Found opioids (43% initiation drug), mean age 21.4 years, peer pressure (38%) as primary initiation factor. | Substance Abuse |
| 2004 | CAT ratification | Convention Against Torture ratified (no reservations). | International Law |
| 2004 | Indian Ocean Tsunami (26 December) | 82 dead, 26 missing; 14 islands permanently evacuated; damage at 62% of GDP. Catalyses expansion of UNICEF child protection programming and international development assistance. | Disaster, Cross-Cutting |
| 2004 | Housing Development Finance Corporation (HDFC) established | Government entity for housing finance on favourable terms; later partially privatised (49/51 government-private). | Housing |
| 2004 | Maldives contribution to UN SG study on VAW | First documentation of domestic violence prevalence and legal/policy context. | Gender, GBV |
| 2005 | "Rights Side of Life" baseline survey (first round) | HRCM/UNDP/FPA survey of public attitudes, awareness, and perceptions on human rights. Subsequent rounds in 2011 and 2019. | Human Rights |
| 2006 | Child Protection Procedure Manual | First systematic child protection guidance, modelled on England's "Working Together" framework. | Child Protection |
| 2006 | ICCPR ratification | International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights ratified (no reservations). | International Law |
| 2006 | ICESCR ratification | International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights ratified (no reservations). | International Law |
| 2006 | First Advanced Certificate in Social Work | Groups of school leavers trained at University of Kelaniya, Sri Lanka. Deployed to FCSCs on atolls. | Social Work, Child Protection |
| 2006 | Maldives Study on Women's Health and Life Experiences | National DV prevalence study (Fulu, 2006). Approximately 1 in 3 women experienced physical/sexual IPV. Foundation for subsequent legislative reform. | Gender, GBV |
| 2006 | Health Master Plan 2006-2015 | Second long-term health sector strategic plan. | Health |
| 2007 | Advanced Diploma in Social Service Work introduced | Intermediate qualification before full BSW degree programme established at MNU. | Social Work |
| 2007 | Fulu (2007) Women's Health and Life Experiences Study | Full WHO multi-country methodology prevalence study; foundational evidence base for DV/GBV policy. | Gender, GBV |
| 2007 | National Adaptation Programme of Action (NAPA) | Climate change adaptation framework; documented observed sea-level trends at Hulhule. | Climate |

### Democratic Transition (2008-2012)

| Year | Legislation / Policy / Milestone | Key Provisions | Sector |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------|--------|
| 2008 | Constitution of the Republic of Maldives | Ratified 7 August 2008. Multi-party democracy, separation of powers, fundamental rights chapter. Article 17 (equality), Article 23 (socioeconomic rights), Article 35 (children's rights), Article 36 (state obligations for vulnerable groups), Article 10 (Islam as state religion). Article 68 requires ratified treaties to be given effect domestically. Article 93 clarifies treaties not directly enforceable without domestic legislation. | Constitutional Law |
| 2008 | Employment Act (Act No. 2/2008) | Labour relations law: working conditions, hours, leave, prohibition of gender discrimination, occupational safety, migrant worker provisions. Minimum wage first established in January 2022 under this Act. | Employment, Gender |
| 2008 | National Child Helpline (1412) established | Toll-free 24-hour child abuse reporting channel, in partnership with Child Helpline International. Given statutory basis in CRPA 2019 Article 76. | Child Protection |
| 2008 | National Housing Policy | Six focus areas: adequate shelter, affordability, housing finance, land management, building standards, institutional coordination. | Housing |
| 2008 | "Madhana" health insurance introduced | Social health insurance scheme for civil servants, pensioners, senior citizens, persons below poverty line, persons with disabilities (~61,000 people, 19% of population). Coverage up to MVR 2,000/year. | Health, Social Protection |
| 2008 | NSPA established | National Social Protection Agency created (initially as department within Ministry of Health and Family) to coordinate social assistance programmes. | Social Protection |
| 2008 | Drug Control Master Plan 2008-2012 | UNODC-assisted national drug control strategic plan. | Substance Abuse |
| 2008 | Biological and Behavioural Survey (BBS) | Estimated 375 injecting drug users in Male and Addu. | Substance Abuse |
| 2009 | Maldives Pension Act (Act No. 8/2009) | Established the Maldives Pension Administration Office (MPAO); created the Old-Age Basic Pension (MVR 2,000/month, later increased to MVR 5,000) and the Maldives Retirement Pension Scheme (MRPS -- defined contribution, mandatory for all employees). | Social Protection, Elderly |
| 2009 | Special Provisions Act to Deal with Child Sex Abuse Offenders (Act No. 12/2009) | Enhanced penalties for child sexual abuse (7-14 years imprisonment); lowered evidentiary standards; mandated Child Sex Offenders Registry; provisions for victim compensation. | Child Protection, Criminal Justice |
| 2009 | Maldivian Red Crescent established | Community capacity for emergency preparedness and response. | Health, Disaster |
| 2009 | Underwater Cabinet Meeting | President Nasheed's underwater cabinet meeting to highlight climate urgency before Copenhagen negotiations. | Climate |
| 2009 | Baby death case | Watershed child protection case exposing systemic failures in inter-agency coordination, supervision, and risk assessment. Led to recommendations for supervisory systems for social workers. | Child Protection |
| 2009 | DHS 2009 | First nationally representative Demographic and Health Survey for the Maldives. | Health, Demographics |
| 2010 | Disability Act (Act No. 8/2010) | Rights of persons with disabilities; established monthly disability allowance (initially MVR 2,000/month); created National Disability Registry; established Disability Council; provisions for education, healthcare, employment access. | Disability |
| 2010 | CRPD ratification | Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities ratified (no reservations). | Disability, International Law |
| 2010 | Decentralisation Act (Act No. 7/2010) | Replaced centralised atoll chief system with elected local councils. Three-to-four atolls grouped into seven administrative provinces. Delegated some social service functions to local councils. CFPS centralised to manage all FCSCs across 20 atolls. | Governance, Decentralisation |
| 2010 | Community Social Groups (CSGs) initiated | Community-led approach to social problems; supported by UNICEF. Revived 2016 under IBAMA project. | Child Protection, Community Development |
| 2010 | "Madhana Plus" introduced | Expanded health insurance to include international medical services; annual contributions of MVR 1,500; scheme covered 61,861 beneficiaries, expenditure MVR 145 million. | Health, Social Protection |
| 2010 | Tobacco Control Act (Act No. 15/2010) | Tobacco control legislation for the Maldives. | Health |
| 2010 | HRCM Disability Baseline Assessment | In partnership with UNDP; assessed activities addressing rights of persons with disabilities. | Disability |
| 2010 | UNODC Country Project signed | "Strengthening the National Response to Combat Drug Abuse in Maldives." | Substance Abuse |
| 2011 | Drugs Act (Act No. 17/2011) | Comprehensive drug law reform. Established National Drug Agency (NDA); created Drug Court; introduced treatment diversion for drug use offences; maintained severe trafficking penalties. Seven treatment facilities established by 2021. | Substance Abuse |
| 2011 | National Social Health Insurance Act (Act No. 15/2011) | Legal basis for universal health insurance (Aasandha). All Maldivian citizens covered; no premiums, co-payments, or deductibles. | Health, Social Protection |
| 2011 | Maldives National University (MNU) established | Upgraded from MCHE to full university status, incorporating the Faculty of Health Sciences (BSW programme) and other faculties. | Education |
| 2011 | "Rights Side of Life" survey (second round) | Updated HRCM/UNDP human rights baseline survey. | Human Rights |
| 2011 | Regulation 2011/R-3 (Disability) | Implementing regulation defining parameters for disability registration and categories. | Disability |
| 2011 | National Drug Use Survey 2011/12 | Updated national drug survey; found one-third of people with drug addiction reported mental health conditions. | Substance Abuse, Mental Health |

### Political Instability and Consolidation (2012-2018)

| Year | Legislation / Policy / Milestone | Key Provisions | Sector |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------|--------|
| 2012 | Domestic Violence Prevention Act (Act No. 3/2012) | Landmark DV legislation. Defines DV broadly (physical, sexual, emotional, verbal, financial, psychological abuse). Mandatory reporting duty on all persons. Created Family Protection Authority (FPA). Protection Orders (general and emergency). Police warrantless entry powers. Does NOT specifically criminalise DV as a standalone offence. | Gender, GBV |
| 2012 | Aasandha launched (January 2012) | Universal health insurance replacing Madhana/Madhana Plus. Initially MVR 100,000/year cap per citizen. | Health, Social Protection |
| 2012 | Thalassaemia Control Act (2012) | National programme for thalassaemia prevention; Maldives has an 18% carrier rate. | Health |
| 2012 | ADB Social Protection Index study | Identified 20 distinct social protection programmes. Total SP expenditure MVR 782 million (3.5% of GDP). Social assistance 52%, social insurance 47%, labour market programmes 1%. | Social Protection |
| 2013 | Family Act First Amendment (Act No. 11/2013) | Amendments to marriage, divorce, and custody provisions. | Family Law |
| 2013 | Prevention of Human Trafficking Act (Act No. 12/2013) | Criminalises all forms of trafficking (forced labour, sexual exploitation, debt bondage). Penalties up to 15 years' imprisonment. Victim identification and protection provisions. Anti-Human Trafficking Office established. | Human Trafficking, Employment |
| 2013 | Anti-Torture Act (2013) | Recognised torture as a crime; conditions punishment on hospitalisation duration. | Human Rights, Criminal Justice |
| 2013 | Minimum Standards for Accessibility (Regulation 2013/R-557) | Accessibility standards for education institutions, health facilities, therapy centres, and government buildings housing people under state care. Narrow scope; does not cover all public or private buildings. | Disability |
| 2014 | Penal Code (Act No. 6/2014) | Comprehensive criminal code. Section 411(d): 100 lashes for extramarital sex (zina); Section 130(b): rebuttable presumption of consent within marriage; death penalty provisions for murder and apostasy. ~85% of persons sentenced to flogging are women and girls. | Criminal Justice, Gender |
| 2014 | Sexual Offences Act (Act No. 17/2014) | First standalone criminalisation of rape, sexual assault, sexual harassment. Defines consent. Section 52 requires five types of evidence for rape prosecution. Section 53 allows delayed reporting to block proceedings. Admits past sexual behaviour evidence. Amended 2021 (Act No. 25/2021). | Gender, GBV, Criminal Justice |
| 2014 | Prevention of Sexual Harassment and Abuse Act (Act No. 16/2014) | Requires all employers to have written sexual harassment policies and complaint committees. Sexual Harassment Offences Committee established nationally. | Gender, Employment |
| 2014 | Social Protection Act (Act No. 2/2014) | Formalised cash transfer programmes: single parent allowance (MVR 1,000/month per child, max MVR 3,000), foster parent allowance, disability allowance, food and utility subsidies. Legal basis for NSPA. | Social Protection |
| 2014 | "Husnuvaa Aasandha" (Unlimited Aasandha) | Removed annual benefit cap; genuinely unlimited government-funded health coverage for all citizens. | Health, Social Protection |
| 2014 | Health Master Plan 2016-2025 published | Third long-term health strategic plan. Vision of population health equity. 12 national-level outputs. Three strategic domains: governance, public health protection, health care delivery. | Health |
| 2015 | Health Services Act (Act No. 29/2015) | Regulatory framework for all health service provision including mental health. | Health |
| 2015 | Health Care Profession Act (Act No. 13/2015) | Regulates health professionals: registration, licensing, practice standards for psychiatrists, psychologists, counsellors, and others. | Health |
| 2015 | National Mental Health Policy 2015-2025 | First comprehensive mental health policy. Six objectives including PHC integration, human resource development, community-based services, human rights protection, information systems, stigma reduction. Based on WHO mhGAP. | Mental Health |
| 2015 | Child protection focal points in schools | Ministry of Education placed designated staff in schools for child protection identification and response. | Child Protection, Education |
| 2015 | Incheon Strategy indicators reporting | Maldives reported on disability data reliability/comparability targets (Goal 8). | Disability |
| 2016 | Gender Equality Act (Act No. 18/2016) | Comprehensive gender equality law. Prohibits gender-based discrimination. Recognises GBV as discrimination. Mandates gender mainstreaming across government. Equal pay for equal work. Framework for GEAP. Complaint mechanisms via HRCM/courts. | Gender |
| 2016 | Family Act Second Amendment (Act No. 9/2016) | Introduced possibility of equitable property division via prenuptial agreements. By 2026, approximately eight prenuptial agreements had ever been made. | Family Law, Gender |
| 2016 | Maldives Child Protection Database (MCPD) launched | Digital case management system linking all 19 FCSCs to centralised CFPS. First national-level child protection data capability. | Child Protection |
| 2016 | CSGs revived under IBAMA project | "You and I Together" -- community-level child protection capacity through UNICEF/MoGFSS and LGA. | Child Protection, Community Development |
| 2016-17 | Demographic and Health Survey (DHS) 2016-17 | Nationally representative survey: fertility, family planning, FGM/C prevalence (13% of women 15-49), spousal violence indicators, nutrition, child health. | Health, Demographics |
| 2018 | National Elderly Policy | First dedicated policy for older persons. Six fundamental rights (dignity, health, work, property/inheritance, social security, freedom from violence). Four core principles (protection, independence/participation, health/well-being, preparatory measures). | Elderly |

### The Solih Administration and Beyond (2019-Present)

| Year | Legislation / Policy / Milestone | Key Provisions | Sector |
|------|----------------------------------|----------------|--------|
| 2019 | Child Rights Protection Act (Act No. 19/2019) | Most comprehensive child protection legislation. Definitions of abuse (physical, sexual, emotional, neglect, exploitation). Mandatory reporting (criminal offence for failure: 2-5 years). Judicial orders (Interim Assessment, Protection, Supervision). Children's Ombudsperson. Marriage age raised to 18 (Family Act loophole remains). Prohibits child labour under 16. 1412 helpline given statutory basis. | Child Protection |
| 2019 | Juvenile Justice Act (Act No. 18/2019) | Age of criminal responsibility raised from 10 to 15. Death penalty prohibited for juveniles. Rehabilitation emphasis. Does NOT abolish flogging. | Child Protection, Criminal Justice |
| 2019 | National Centre for Mental Health established (IGMH) | Inaugurated by President Solih. Outpatient, inpatient (wards opened 2022), and coordination of regional services. Reduced psychiatry waitlists from 3,300+ to ~330 (90% reduction by 2023). | Mental Health |
| 2019 | Strategic Action Plan 2019-2023 (SAP) | Government-wide strategic plan. Assigned MoGFSS lead role in integrated social service delivery. Targets for prison population reduction (20%), rehabilitation participation increase (50%). | Governance, Cross-Cutting |
| 2019 | HIES 2019/20 | Household Income and Expenditure Survey. National poverty rate 5.4%. Atolls 9.5%, Male 0.9%. National Gini 29.3. Disability prevalence 9% (WG-SS methodology). | Poverty, Demographics |
| 2019 | "Rights Side of Life" survey (third round) | Updated HRCM/UNDP human rights baseline. | Human Rights |
| 2019 | National Plan of Action on Older Persons 2019-2023 | Operationalises Elderly Policy: 34 priority actions, 10 stakeholder agencies, 4 policy areas (care/support, independence/participation, healthcare/wellbeing, preparation for ageing). Implementation disrupted by COVID-19. | Elderly |
| 2019 | "Merana" programme (Aasandha overseas treatment) | Coverage at partner hospitals in India, Sri Lanka, Malaysia for conditions not treatable domestically. | Health |
| 2019 | Education Sector Plan 2019-2023 | UNESCO-supported plan: improving learning outcomes, reducing geographic disparities, strengthening TVET, ICT integration. | Education |
| 2019 | Maldives published first MPI | Multi-Dimensional Poverty Index based on DHS 2017 data. Male deprivation driven by congestion/healthcare; atoll deprivation by education/water/sanitation. | Poverty |
| 2020 | Education Act (Act No. 24/2020) | First comprehensive education law. Compulsory education ages 4-16. Free education through Grade 12. Inclusive education mandate. Teacher qualification standards. Explicit ban on corporal punishment in schools. | Education |
| 2020 | Children's Ombudsperson's Office established | Under CRPA 2019; independent monitoring, investigation, and advocacy for children's rights. | Child Protection |
| 2020 | Alternative Care Regulation (2020-R-69) | Governs foster care, kinship care, and residential care placements. Only 18 registered foster carers (2023), caring for 16 children; 91% of 187 children in alternative care are in residential institutional settings. | Child Protection |
| 2020 | Regulation on Reintegration (2020/R-30) | Addresses reintegration of children from alternative care into family/community. | Child Protection |
| 2020 | NAP on Preventing and Countering Violent Extremism 2020-2024 | NCTC/UNDP "whole of society" approach. Five strategic objectives targeting youth resilience, care, education, shared values, and engagement of isolated groups/individuals. | Security, Youth |
| 2020 | WHO STEPS Survey 2020-21 | NCD risk factor surveillance. | Health |
| 2020 | COVID-19 pandemic response begins | School closures, lockdowns, GBV Sub-Cluster activated, Income Support Allowance. GDP contracted 33.6%. Tourism halt. Rapid Livelihood Assessment: 44% reported redundancy/income loss. | Cross-Cutting |
| 2020 | Geveshi Gulhun campaign | National campaign to combat domestic violence during COVID-19 lockdowns. | Gender, GBV |
| 2021 | Sexual Offences Act Amendment (Act No. 25/2021) | Harsher sentences, closed loopholes, limited criminalisation of marital rape in certain circumstances. Marital rape remains "incompletely criminalised" due to Penal Code Section 130(b). | Gender, GBV, Criminal Justice |
| 2021 | Regulation 2021/R-54 (Disability Registry) | Updated criteria for registration in the National Disability Registry. | Disability |
| 2021 | NDA/WHO Situational Analysis of Drugs | Most comprehensive recent drug situation assessment: police, customs, courts, correctional services, and health data. | Substance Abuse |
| 2021 | Mental Health Strategic Plan 2021-2025 | Central and Regional Mental Health Services Development Plan 2022-2025. Establishment of Mental Health Centres at regional hospitals. | Mental Health |
| 2022 | Minimum wage established (January 2022) | First minimum wage under the Employment Act 2008. | Employment |
| 2022 | Population and Housing Census 2022 | Total population ~521,000. 24,401 persons with disabilities (7%). TFR 1.7. Foreign workers ~132,493 (26% of residents). 47% of population in Male. | Demographics |
| 2022 | Gender Equality Action Plan (GEAP) 2022-2026 | Launched March 2022 with Steering Committee chaired by the President. Operationalises Gender Equality Act 2016. | Gender |
| 2022 | National Social Protection Framework 2022-2026 | World Bank/UNICEF-supported. Four pillars: lifecycle protection, shock responsiveness, institutional strengthening, digital transformation. | Social Protection |
| 2022 | Child Rights Symposium | Co-hosted by Children's Ombudsperson and UNICEF; focused on inter-agency coordination for child protection. | Child Protection |
| 2022 | Mental Health Inpatient Wards opened (IGMH) | Dedicated psychiatric inpatient capacity including padded observation rooms. | Mental Health |
| 2022 | Hulhumale Hospital Mental Health Centre | Established October 2022 in collaboration with Cadabams Group (India). | Mental Health |
| 2022 | ICSVE Prison Radicalisation Study | Dr. Anne Speckhard study at Maafushi Prison. Documented structured extremist recruitment, gang-extremism nexus, drug-to-extremism pipeline. | Security, Criminal Justice |
| 2023 | Policy on Allowance for Persons with Disabilities | Tiered allowance structure: basic MVR 2,000 + general additional MVR 1,000 + carer MVR 2,000 + self-care MVR 1,000. Maximum MVR 7,000/month. | Disability |
| 2023 | UNFPA Comprehensive Legal Review (GBV laws) | McCabe (2023): identified "repetitions, overlaps, contradictions and gaps" across GBV legal architecture; confirmed DV not specifically criminalised; marital rape incompletely criminalised. | Gender, GBV |
| 2023 | HRCM CRPD Shadow Report | 57-page submission identifying gaps between disability legislation and implementation across education, accessibility, employment, healthcare, DV protections. | Disability, Human Rights |
| 2023 | Muizzu administration begins | Ministry restructured as Ministry of Social and Family Development (MoSFD). | Governance |
| 2023 | Transparency Maldives Health Sector Corruption Report | Identified 35 corruption vulnerabilities across Maldivian health system procurement and governance. | Health |
| 2024 | Regulation on Employment of Persons with Disabilities (2024/R-9) | Separate employment registry for PWDs; mandates competency profiles, reasonable accommodations, and needs assessments by employers. | Disability, Employment |
| 2024 | Maldives Police Service Strategic Plan 2024-2028 | DV identified as strategic priority alongside organised crime, drug trafficking, human trafficking, cybercrime, terrorism. | Criminal Justice, GBV |
| 2024 | UNPRPD Situational Analysis (Faisal, 2024) | Most comprehensive recent disability assessment. Documented systemic failures in equality, accessibility, inclusive service delivery, and budgeting. | Disability |
| 2024 | Zakat House integrated into OneGov platform | Cross-referencing with NSPA database of social assistance recipients. | Social Protection |
| 2025 | Maldives Mental Health Strategic Action Plan 2025-2029 | Latest mental health strategic framework with time-bound targets for service expansion, workforce development, and PHC integration. | Mental Health |
| 2025 | Rogers, Ali & Naeem child protection systems analysis | Most detailed academic analysis of child protection system development, procedural frameworks, and frontline realities. Published January 2025. | Child Protection |
